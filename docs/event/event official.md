🚀 Why this hackathon?
This hackathon is an event where, by tackling YC's published Request for Startups (RFS) - Summer 2026, you can experience in a single day the thinking and standards YC looks for.

Rather than a hypothetical problem, we offer the experience of tackling an area that investors and entrepreneurs around the world are focusing on right now, by global standards.

Within a limited amount of time, you will find an important problem, form a hypothesis, and shape it into a product — through this entire process, this is a place to take your first step toward challenging YC.

🔸 What is Y Combinator (YC)?
YC is the world's top accelerator, investing $500,000 (approximately ¥75 million) in every startup that wins its competitive selection process, and providing hands-on mentoring and a network of 10,000 founders.

Since 2005, it has invested in more than 5,000 companies, with a cumulative market capitalization of approximately ¥120 trillion.
It has produced more than 100 unicorn companies, generating numerous world-class companies such as Airbnb, Stripe, Dropbox, Zapier, Coinbase, and Deel.

In addition, Sam Altman, who led OpenAI — the developer of ChatGPT — is an early YC alumnus and former CEO of YC.

💡 This Theme - RFS Summer 2026
The Request for Startups (RFS) is a published list of startup areas that YC believes currently have “the greatest market opportunity.”

On the day of the event, teams will choose one of the following three themes and develop a product.

1. Company Brain — Tom Blomfield
A system that structures and updates fragmented internal company knowledge and converts it into an execution layer for AI.

🔗 YC RFS Page
https://www.ycombinator.com/rfs/?status=new#company-brain

2. Dynamic Software Interfaces — Ankit Gupta
Software interfaces that users and coding agents can radically customize to fit their own workflows.

🔗 YC RFS Page
https://www.ycombinator.com/rfs/?status=new#dynamic-software-interfaces

3. Software for Agents — Aaron Epstein
Agent-first software designed around machine-readable interfaces such as APIs, MCP, CLI, and strong documentation.

🔗 YC RFS Page
https://www.ycombinator.com/rfs/?status=new#software-for-agents

🤝 Organizers & Partners
- Transpose Platform
As a Significant Investor in Y Combinator (YC), one of the world's leading accelerators, Transpose Platform supports Japanese entrepreneurs, students, and young researchers in taking on the global market and building the ability to compete by global standards.

- Ritsumeikan University
Ritsumeikan University is one of the largest comprehensive universities in the Kansai region, with campuses in Kyoto, Shiga, and Osaka.

With approximately 38,000 undergraduate students and 4,000 graduate students, it has a broad education and research foundation spanning 16 faculties and 21 graduate schools across the sciences and humanities — including Information Science and Engineering, Science and Engineering, Life Sciences, Pharmacy, Business Administration, Policy Science, International Relations, Image Arts and Sciences, and Food Management — as well as an international partnership network of 463 institutions across 68 countries and regions.

- OUVC (Osaka University Venture Capital)
OUVC (Osaka University Venture Capital) was established in 2014, wholly funded by Osaka University, and is one of the leading university-affiliated VCs in the Kansai region, managing a cumulative fund of ¥23.1 billion.

Not limited to startups originating from Osaka University, it invests in and supports the growth of university-originated startups nationwide, with a focus on deep-tech fields such as bio/healthcare, AI/robotics, and fusion/quantum technology. It supports, both financially and managerially, startups that are working to bring the world-class technologies held by Japanese universities into practical social use, so they can succeed on the global stage.

🛠️ AI Tools to Be Used - GStack / GBrain by Garry Tan (CEO of YC)
GStack / GBrain are tools developed by YC CEO Garry Tan that let AI understand the flow of development and work with context in mind, without needing detailed instructions from a human every time.

GStack: A set of procedures for advancing through specification organization, design, implementation, QA, and documentation.

GBrain: A notepad that helps AI remember a project's rules and past decisions.

In this hackathon, teams will develop their products using AI development tools and the GStack / GBrain concepts, in order to shape a product in a short amount of time.
